Nachenka~Ukranian Spoon Bread

This is my favourite "cornbread" recipe, probably because it is almost nothing like the corbread from the southwest, instead originating in eastern Europe. My family calls it Nachenka or Ukranian Spoon Bread, and I have also seen it called Ukranian Baked Cornmeal. I think the "spoon bread" name comes from the fact that because it is so moist and soft, you can't really eat it with your hands like other cornbreads, and we tend to scoop it onto our plates with spoons. This texture is one of Nachenka's unique features, the others being the bright yellow colour that is preserved through the cooking process, and the distinct, buttery flavour. I find Nachenka to be less sweet than other cornbread, and the sauteed onion, butter and heavy cream make this a soft, delicious savoury treat.
